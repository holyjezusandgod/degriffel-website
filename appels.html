De Griffel telde rond de 180 appelrassen, geënt over vier jaar. Hieronder de 125 waarvan we foto's en historische gegevens hebben kunnen samenbrengen — van Vlaamse streekrassen tot 19e-eeuwse Engelse keukenappels en zeldzame Duitse Reinetten. Veel van deze rassen groeien vandaag verder in Noord-Frankrijk en bij Seppe.

De beschrijvingen komen uit de National Fruit Collection in Brogdale (UK). Klik op een ras voor de volledige geschiedenis en vruchtbeschrijving.

Baldwin

LaatEetUSA
Sweet, fruity, retaining crisp, lively character; but often fails to ripen in England.
Volledige beschrijving
GeschiedenisFound c1740 on farm of John Ball, Wilmington, Massachusetts. Originally Pecker or Woodpecker, because the tree was frequented by woodpeckers. Popularised, renamed early 1800s. Stone apple on pillar, erected in 1895, marks ‘the estate where in 1793 Saml. Thompson Esq, while locating the line of the Middlesex canal discovered the first Pecker Apple tree later named the Baldwin’. Colonel Loammi Baldwin was also an engineer on the Middlesex Canal and his statue at North Woburn is wreathed in apples and inscribed ‘Disseminator of the apple in honor of him called the Baldwin apple, which proceeds from a tree growing wild about 2 miles north of this monument’. Overtaken by Jonathan in early 1900s; but still popular in the northeastern US.
VruchtSweet, fruity, retaining crisp, lively character; but often fails to ripen in England. Highly esteemed and widely grown across northern States in C19th; exported to UK. Dumelow’s Seedling

LaatStoofUK
Pale greenish white to pale yellow, sometimes mottled with bright apricot or stripes.
Volledige beschrijving
SynoniemenSyn Wellington
GeschiedenisRaised in the late 1700s by Richard Dummeller, a farmer in Shakerstone, near Ashby-de-la-Zouche, Leicestershire. Believed to come from a Northern Greening pip. Sent from Gopsall Hall (Earl Howe’s estate) to Richard Williams, who exhibited it in 1818 at the London Horticultural Society as Dumelow’s Crab. Renamed Wellington around 1819 or 1820.
